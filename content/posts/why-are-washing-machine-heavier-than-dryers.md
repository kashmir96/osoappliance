When it comes to laundry appliances, washing machines are usually heavier than dryers. This is because washing machines use more energy and require more components than dryers.

Washing machines use more energy than dryers because they use a combination of hot and cold water to clean clothes. The hot water helps to break down dirt and grime, while the cold water helps to rinse the clothes. This process requires more energy than the dryer, which simply uses hot air to dry the clothes.

In addition to needing more energy, washing machines also require more components than dryers. Washing machines contain a motor, a pump, a timer, and several other components that are used to move the clothes through the wash cycle. Dryers, on the other hand, only need a motor and a timer. All of these components add to the weight of the washing machine, making it heavier than the dryer.

Finally, washing machines are usually made from heavier materials than dryers. This is because washing machines need to be able to withstand more wear and tear than dryers. Washing machines are often made from stainless steel or other heavy-duty metals, while dryers are usually made from plastic or other lightweight materials.
