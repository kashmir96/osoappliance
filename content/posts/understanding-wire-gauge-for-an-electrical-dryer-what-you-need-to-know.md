## What You Need to Know about Wire Gauge for an Electrical Dryer
Having a functional and efficient Dryer is very important for any household. Generally, dryers have a heavy load and require the correct wiring connection to ensure that it runs smoothly and safely. Understanding the wire gauge for an electrical dryer is key in ensuring it’s properly installed and maintained. 

Wire gauge is the unit of measure used to identify the diameter of the wire in your electrical connection. In other words, it's the size of the wire. With electrical dryers, it's important to use the right wire gauge as the wrong one may not be able to safely support the load. This could lead to further complications and potentially a fire hazard. 

Different fuels used with dryers require different wire gauges. Generally, a gas dryer requires smaller wire gauge than an electric one. National Electric Code (NEC) is the governing body that specifies the gauge of wire size needed for a dryer. If a local code dictates a different wire gauge than NEC, then it is important to follow the local one.

When dealing with wire gauge for an electrical dryer it is also important to remember that there needs to be a dedicated circuit for the dryer. All wiring for the drying should be dedicated for the single appliance. This extends to the breaker and should not run with other items on the circuit.

At the end of the day, the key to understanding wire gauge for an electrical dryer is to make sure the wiring is up to code. or up to local specifications. Do not take chances with your safety and the safety of your family. If in doubt, consult your electrician or a certified technician.
