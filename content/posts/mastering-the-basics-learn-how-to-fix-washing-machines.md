## Improve Your Maintenance Skills to Repair Washing Machines
Homeowners who rely on washing machines for clean laundry may find themselves frustrated when the appliance breaks down. Fortunately, there are steps that novices and experienced DIYers alike can take to master some of the basics of washing machine repair.

The first step to fixing a washing machine at home is to shut off the hot and cold water to the appliance. Then, the user should unplug the washer and access the pump area by removing the hoses. From there, it is important to check for any clogged hoses and properly clean them. Additionally, the belt must be inspected and replaced if necessary.

Ensuring a washing machine is level is another important part of maintenance. Many homeowners find themselves needing to adjust and level the machine periodically to ensure it functions properly. Looking for signs of friction, noise, and vibration while running the washer can also be a helpful indicator on whether or not the machine is balanced properly.

The user should also remember to frequently clear out the lint filter, which can often become clogged over time. Furthermore, replacing the hoses and hose washers every 3-5 years can prevent some common problems that a washing machine faces. Lastly, it is important to fill up a sink or bucket with hot water and bleach to clear out any mold and grime from the washtub.

By following these tips and mastering the basics of washing machine repair, homeowners can avoid more serious problems in the future and save money. If significant repairs are needed, it is best to consult a professional. The ultimate goal is to keep the washing machine in good condition for as long as possible.
