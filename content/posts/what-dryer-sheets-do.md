Dryer sheets are a common household essential used to make laundry smell great and soften fabric. But did you know that dryer sheets also have a few other uses? Read on to learn more about what dryer sheets do and why they're so important for your laundry.

Dryer sheets are designed to be used during the drying cycle of the washing machine. They are typically cotton or polyester-based sheets that contain ingredients such as softeners, fragrances, and anti-static agents. When placed in the dryer with wet laundry, these ingredients help reduce static cling and make fabrics softer. They also help reduce wrinkles in fabrics and add a pleasant scent to your clothes.

In addition to making your clothes smell good and feel soft, dryer sheets can also be used for cleaning purposes. You can use them to clean windows, mirrors, and other surfaces by adding a few drops of water to the sheet before wiping down the surface. Dryer sheets can also be used for dusting furniture, removing pet hair from carpets and upholstery, and even cleaning car interiors!

Lastly, dryer sheets are great for repelling insects such as mosquitoes or gnats in outdoor spaces like your patio or deck. Simply place one or two dryer sheets in an area where you tend to get bitten by bugs; the fragrance will help keep them away!
